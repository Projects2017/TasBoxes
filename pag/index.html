Because of as steady stream of reliable high percentage set ups you actually have quite a bit of freedom as to how you would like to manage your exits.

In order to provide you some guidance…here is how I do it:

You see…I hate losers and losing money. I hate drawdowns in my account..and I do everything to eliminate them or keep them as shallow as possible.

There are a few rules I follow to max out my winning percentage, avoid excessive drawdowns and in the unfortunate even that I take a loss….the loss does not hurt me.

Rule # 1: I use a Money Management Strategy to raise my overall winning percentage. The way it works is that I exit half of my position at a profit point which equals the amount of stop price. This provides me a risk free trade. This way I can let the profits run without having to worry about the trade ever becoming a looser. As an example…I buy 100 IBM shares at 171.17. My stop is at 169.87 ( a potential loss of $ 1.30 per share)…I will get out of 50 shares (half my position) at a price point 1.30 above my entry. (price of 172.47 in this example). This simple strategy dramatically reduces my losing percentage. As I said before…I hate loosing money, this simple money management exit ensure that I avoid excessive drawdowns.

Rule # 2: Once I am out of 50 % of the stock I concentrate on managing the trade. Generally speaking I look to take a profit which is at least 3 to 5 times greater than the initial stop.

Rule # 3: If the stock is in a running mode (making new highs every day in a case where I am long or new lows in a case where I am short) I will simply raise my stop below the previous day’s low and follow the market up.

The rules described above are the ones which work for me. They suit my personality and trading style. They work for me.

However…different people have different styles…I encourage you to use what works for you. As long as you have some patience to let your winners run…you will be ok.
